Thoughts on "19 Ways to Acquire Customers"

CLICK HERE TO VIEW "19 Ways to Acquire Customers"


I just read something that really got me thinking. It was all about promoting a product and building a user base. Honestly, I’ve never tried to get people to buy anything before, so this whole topic was kind of new territory for me. Up until now, my launch plan was pretty simple. I figured I’d introduce my product in some groups where I already know people would be interested. It made sense at the time; just go to the spaces where my audience already exists and say, “Hey, check this out!” and while that might’ve worked to get the ball rolling, the more I thought about it, the more I realized that would only take me so far. I’d probably hit a ceiling at a few hundred users, maybe less.


So now, I’m starting to see that I need to think bigger. One thing that stood out to me was how powerful blogging can be, not just writing my own posts, but also getting my product mentioned on other blogs that already have a loyal audience. I know for a fact that the crowd I’m trying to reach loves reading about how to write better. If I can create or contribute to blogs that offer real value in that space, I can naturally introduce my software tool as something useful. Not in a pushy “buy this now” kind of way, but more like, “Here’s something that might make your writing life easier.”


That kind of approach feels more genuine to me. I don’t really want to be a salesperson; I want to build something helpful and have it find the people who actually need it. Blogging gives me a way to do that. And when you pair that with good SEO, it becomes even more powerful. I wouldn’t have to rely solely on word of mouth or existing communities. Instead, I’d be creating content that lives on and keeps bringing people in over time.


Getting featured on other blogs is another part of the puzzle I hadn’t really considered before. If I can build relationships with bloggers who already have trust and credibility in my niche, that opens the door to a whole new level of exposure. Their readers are already listening, and if they vouch for what I’m building, it could go a long way.


So now, I’m rethinking my whole strategy. Instead of just aiming for a few hundred users, I’m starting to imagine what it could look like to reach thousands or more. There’s still a lot to figure out, but at least now I’ve got a clearer picture of how I might get there.


Thanks for reading!
